Hong Kong's MTR metro system may close public operations just after 1 a.m. each day, but that doesn't mean the system goes to sleep. Each night, after the last trains have departed the stations, thousands of workers enter the dark tunnels to inspect, maintain and replace the tracks before the system returns to service at dawn.
